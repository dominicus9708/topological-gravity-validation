SN2014J_late_time_sources.txt
=============================

Primary late-time source
------------------------
[LT-01]
Author: Or Graur
Year: 2018
Title: Late-time observations of the type Ia supernova SN 2014J with the Hubble Space Telescope Wide Field Camera 3
Journal: The Astrophysical Journal
DOI: 10.3847/1538-4357/aaf1c3

Why this matters
----------------
- Covers SN 2014J at approximately 360 to 1300 days past maximum light.
- Specifically useful for extending the observation window beyond the ~400-day direct-table segment from Yang et al. 2017.
- States that even though SN 2014J has a resolved light echo, the late-time photometry is not contaminated by an unresolved echo.

Use role in project
-------------------
- late-time extension source
- photometric continuation source
- should be treated separately from direct-table light-echo geometry data

Related standard-range source already in use
--------------------------------------------
[STD-01]
Author: Yang et al.
Year: 2017
Title: Interstellar-medium mapping in M82 through light echoes around supernova 2014J

Why this matters
----------------
- Provides direct-table values for approximately 215.8, 276.5, 365.3, and 415.6 days after B-band maximum.
- Current standard v2 pipeline is based on its Table 3 and Table 4.

Public image-range support
--------------------------
[IMG-01]
Source: NASA / Hubble / STScI public image pages
Window mentioned: 2014-11-06 to 2016-10-12

Why this matters
----------------
- Confirms that public light-echo imaging extends to nearly 1000 days after discovery.
- Useful as broad observational-range support, but not by itself a direct-table measurement source.

Important caution
-----------------
If the late-time segment is added later, it should be stored with a source-quality distinction such as:
- data_origin = direct_table
- data_origin = late_time_photometry
- data_origin = image_extracted

Do not merge the late-time segment into the earlier direct-table geometry segment as if they are the same measurement class.

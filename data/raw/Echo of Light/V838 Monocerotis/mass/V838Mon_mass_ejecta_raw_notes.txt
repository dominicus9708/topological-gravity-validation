V838Mon_mass_ejecta_raw_notes.txt
=================================

What can be used
----------------
The safest mass-related route for V838 Mon is not a full supernova-style mass budget.
Instead, use:

- event character of the 2002 outburst
- ejecta/shell-mass estimates from spectroscopic interpretation
- mass-related values only as auxiliary structural or event-strength constraints

Main supporting source
----------------------
[ME-01] The 2002 outburst of V838 Mon: as cool as it gets
- Useful for the physical character of the 2002 event
- Summarizes multiple brightness peaks and very cool supergiant-like evolution
- Includes shell/ejecta mass context on the order of about 10^-7 to 10^-5 solar masses
- Best treated as an auxiliary raw-source note, not as a final precise total mass budget

Caution
-------
Do not equate:
- the dust responsible for the later light echo
with
- the full mass directly ejected during the 2002 event

Why
---
The direct light-echo literature often favors an interstellar thin-sheet interpretation,
which means the scattering dust is not simply identical to newly ejected stellar material.

Suggested use in topological work
---------------------------------
- treat ejecta/shell-mass estimates as event-strength context
- do not use them as a direct substitute for light-echo dust mass
- keep them as auxiliary bounds when discussing structural reorganization intensity

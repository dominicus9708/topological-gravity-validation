# DiskMass Survey data

## Description
The DiskMass Survey is a survey of nearly face-on spiral galaxies designed to constrain disk mass surface densities using stellar and gas kinematics.

## Core references
Bershady, M. A., Verheijen, M. A. W., Swaters, R. A., et al. (2010),
"The DiskMass Survey. I. Overview",
The Astrophysical Journal, 716, 198.

Martinsson, T. P. K., Verheijen, M. A. W., Westfall, K. B., et al. (2013),
"The DiskMass Survey. VII. The distribution of luminous and dark matter in spiral galaxies",
Astronomy & Astrophysics, 557, A131.

Martinsson, T. P. K., Verheijen, M. A. W., Westfall, K. B., et al. (2013),
"The DiskMass Survey. VI. Gas and stellar kinematics in 30 spiral galaxies",
Astronomy & Astrophysics, 557, A130.

## Use in this project
Used as an auxiliary source for disk-thickness-related dynamical constraints, stellar velocity dispersion context, and alias/crossmatch exploration.

## Notes
DiskMass is primarily a dynamical constraint survey, not a direct edge-on thickness catalog.
